Dr. B. R. Ambedkar

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar popularly known as Babasaheb, was an Indian jurist, economist, politician and social reformer who inspired the Modern Buddhist Movement and campaigned against social discrimination against Untouchables (Dalits), while also supporting the rights of women and labour. He was Independent India's first law minister and the principal architect of the Constitution of India.Ambedkar was a prolific student, earning a law degree and various doctorates from Columbia University and the London School of Economics, and gained a reputation as a scholar for his research in law, economics and political science. In his early career he was an economist, professor, and lawyer. His later life was marked by his political activities; he became involved in campaigning and negotiations for India's independence, publishing journals advocating political rights and social freedom for Dalits, and contributing significantly to the establishment of the state of India.In 1990, the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, was posthumously conferred upon Ambedkar.

Time line of Dr. Ambedkar's life:

  • 1891 - Born at Mahu (Madhya Pradesh, India).
  • 1913 - Passed B.A Examination with Persian and English from University of Bombay.
  • 1915 - Passed M.A. Examination majoring in Economics and with Sociology, History Philosophy, Anthropology and Politics as the other subjects of study.
  • 1916 - Left Colombia University after completing work for the Ph.D, to join the London School of Economics and Political Science, London as a graduate student.
  • 1917 - Columbia University conferred a Degree of Ph.D.
  • 1918 - Professor of Political Economy in the Sydenham College of Commerce & Economics, Bombay.
  • 1924 -Started practice in the Bombay High Court.
  • 1924 - Founded the 'Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha' for the uplift of the depressed classes.
  • 1925 - Published 'The Evolution of Provincial Finance in British India' - dissertation on the provincial decentralisation of Imperial Finance in India'.
  • 1927 - Started a fortnightly Marathi paper Bahiskrit Bharat Dr Ambedkar himself was the editor.
  • 1927 - Established 'Samaj Samata Sangh'.
  • 1930 - Satyagraha at Kalram Temple. Nasik to secure for the Untouchables the right of entry into the temple.
  • 1930-32 - Delegate. Round Table Conference representing Untouchables of India.
  • 1932 - Signed with Mr. M.K. Gandhi the Poona Pact giving up, to save Gandhi's life. separate electorates granted to the Depressed Classes by Ramsay MacDonald's Communal Award, and accepting, instead representation through joint electorates.
  • 1932-34 - Member joint Parliamentary Committee on the Indian Constitutional Reform.
  • 1947 - India obtained her Independence. Dr Ambedkar was elected to the Constituent Assembly by the Bombay Legislature Congress Party. Dr Ambedkar joined Nehru's Cabinet. He became the First Law Minister of Independent India. The Constituent Assembly appointed him to the drafting Committee, which elected him as a Chairman on 29th August 1947.
  • 1948 - Dr Ambedkar completed the Draft Constitution of Indian Republic.
  • 1951 - Dr Ambedkar founded "The Bhartiya Buddha Jansangh".
  • 1952 - Columbia University (USA) conferred the honorary Degree of LL.D., in its Bi-Centennial Celebrations Special Convocation held in New York.
  • 1956 - Died and Cremation at Dadar Chawpatti – Now known as Chaitya Bhoomi Dadar (Bombay).

Conclusion

On December 6, 1956, India lost a great soul and a legendary figure who passed away at his home in Delhi, he was none other than Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, (Dr. B.R. Ambedkar) popularly known as Babasaheb/Ambedkar-saheb. Dr. Babasaheb was born on 14 April 1891; the day in many parts of the country is celebrated as New Year.Time to time many genius persons were born in India and all were not born with silver spoon as many of them had to struggle in their life owing to economic, social and other conditions. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar was one such person who had to fight in his life because of social stigma, not economic. With his knowledge, wisdom and humanitarian feelings he had proved by caste one cannot be great and one can be great only by deeds. He was jurist, economist, politician, social reformer, prolific writer and sociologist as a sequel he could reach to zenith of intellectual excellence. Also he was the architect of our constitution for which we all are proud as almost same time Pakistan was created but within 25 years East Pakistan became Bangladesh and several times Army rule was observed in Pakistan. Dr. Ambedkar contributed substantially to the formulation of post-war economic development plan of India. His views towards family planning and development of agriculture are having great relevance today also. He felt the brunt of caste issue when his father retired from army. In his own words “……my father was employed in the Army. He held the rank of Subedar at that time. Since we lived in the cantonment, we had little to do with the world outside the military area. I had no experience of Untouchability”. But after his father’s retirement situation totally changed as he suffered from serious caste issue which I feel is totally against humanity and deplorable. I wish to cite one example here we all know temple is a place of worship for Hindus so all human beings have the right to visit temple. But in case of the Kalaram Temple of Nasik city Dalits were not allowed to enter, so Dr. Ambedkar-saheb led a protest outside the temple on 2 March 1930 to allow Dalits to enter the temple. Dr. Ambedkar was appointed first law minister in the Cabinet of Pt. Jawaharlal Nehru. What a coincidence as Pakistan’s first Law and Labour Minister was also from Scheduled Caste (Hindu) community, Sri Jogendra Nath Mandal, hailing from undivided Bengal. He was selected by Mohammed Ali Jinnah, First Governor General of Pakistan. However, both of them resigned subsequently – Dr. Ambedkar resigned in September, 1951 and Sri Mandal in October, 1950. Sri Mandal later on migrated to India (then Calcutta, now Kolkata). Dr. Ambedkar-saheb although, passed away on 6 December 1956, leaving behind his second wife Dr. Sharada Kabir, a Saraswat Brahmin, but Indians can never forget him.
